> About Me

I'm a political scientist with a Ph.D. from Binghamton University, specializing in computational social science. My research focuses on political parties, elections, representation, and party systems, where I employ quantitative and computational methods to analyze political behavior and party strategies.

My dissertation, "A Computational Approach to Party Cohesion: When Do Parties Use the Broad-Appeal Strategy in Different Party Systems?", explores party behavior through advanced NLP and topic modeling techniques. I'm passionate about turning complex political questions into testable models and clear data visualizations.
